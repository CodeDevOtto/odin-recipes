Chiretas

Description

Chiretas is a traditional dish from the Spanish Pyrenees, particularly in Arag√≥n. It consists of lamb stomach stuffed with a mixture of rice, diced offal (lung, heart, and liver), bacon, and spices. The stuffed stomach is then sewn shut and boiled until firm. Similar to haggis but without blood, chiretas can be eaten hot or cold and are sometimes fried for extra crispiness. They are often served with bread and red wine.

Ingredients

  • 1 cleaned lamb stomach
  • 200g rice
  • 200g lamb lung
  • 200g lamb heart
  • 200g lamb liver
  • 100g bacon or pork fat
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 teaspoon chopped parsley
  • 1 teaspoon sweet paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pork casing or kitchen thread for tying

Preparation

  1. Clean the stomach:
    • Wash the lamb stomach thoroughly with water and salt.
    • Soak it in water with vinegar for at least 2 hours to remove any strong odors.
  2. Prepare the filling:
    • Boil the lung, heart and liver in salted water for a few minutes.
    • Chop them into small pieces.
    • In a bowl, mix the chopped offal with the rice, diced bacon, crushed garlic, parsley, paprika, salt and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Stuff the chiretas:
    • Cut the stomach into rectangular pieces (about 15 cm / 6 inches long).
    • Fill each piece with the mixture, but don't overfill (the rice will expand while cooking).
    • Sew the edges with kitchen thread or tie with pork casing to create well-sealed packets.
  4. Cooking:
    • In a large pot with salted water, boil the chiretas over medium heat for 1.5 to 2 hours until firm and fully cooked.
    • You can poke them with a needle to prevent bursting.
  5. Serving:
    • Chiretas can be eaten hot or cold.
    • They are often served with bread and red wine or even fried for a crispy texture.
