Crespillos

Description

Crespillos are a traditional dessert from Aragón, particularly in the region of Huesca. These sweet fritters are made with tender spinach or chard leaves, coated in a light batter, and fried until crispy. They are typically enjoyed during spring festivals and are a delicious way to use fresh greens.

Ingredients

  • 200 g (1 ½ cups) all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 250ml milk
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon anise liqueur
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 20 fresh spinach leaves
  • Olive oil

Preparation

  1. In a bowl, whisk the eggs with the sugar.
  2. Add the milk, anise liqueur and a pinch of salt.
  3. Gradually mix in the flour and baking powder until you get a smooth, slightly thick batter.
  4. Dip each spinach leaf into the batter, making sure it's well coated but not dripping.
  5. Heat olive oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat.
  6. Fry the battered leaves until golden and crispy, about 1-2 minutes per side.
  7. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  8. Sprinkle with sugar while still warm.
